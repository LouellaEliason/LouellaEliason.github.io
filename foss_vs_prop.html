My Experiences and Observations with Open Source and Propietary Softwares

My education in GIS has primarily been built using ESRI's collection of Arc softwares and programs. I have used QGIS and Diva GIS, both open source softwares that offer a range of functions similar to ArcGIS Pro. I have noticed that no matter what data format I am able to find, ArcGIS Pro has demonstrated great interoperability functions and allows many files conversions. Most of my experience comes from the university setting, where licensing for students often tends to be cheaper, so while I see the value in a low to zero cost deployability of FOSS, I have been lucky enough to learn and conduct my research using propietary software with important analysis extensions included. My slight experience using FOSS and Paul Ramsey's presentation from a managers perspective has definitely increased the chances I will be using and employing the interoperabilty pathway in my future. I have projects that have been developed using ArcPy or ArcGIS Online that would be tiresome to start over with using FOSS, but I can see the value in using FOSS to enhance my projects in the future.
